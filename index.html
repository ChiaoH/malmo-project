Why Malmö?

Sweden's third largest city right by the Öresund Strait

Malmö has a rich history, dating back to the Middle Ages, and has been shaped by its location on the Öresund Strait, which has made it an important center of trade and industry. Today, Malmö is a vibrant and diverse city known for its innovative architecture, cultural attractions, and excellent quality of life.

Some of the most popular attractions in Malmö include the Turning Torso skyscraper, Malmö Castle, and the Malmö Konsthall art museum. The city is also home to a thriving culinary scene, with many excellent restaurants serving both traditional Swedish cuisine and international dishes.

Top 3 Tourist Attractions

Turning Torso

This striking building is an architectural marvel and the tallest skyscraper in Sweden. It's located in the Western Harbour area of Malmö and offers stunning views of the city and the Øresund Strait.

Address:

Lilla Varvsgatan 14, 211 15 Malmö

Malmö Castle

This historic castle dates back to the 16th century and was once the residence of Danish royalty. Today, it houses a museum showcasing the history of Malmö and its surrounding regions.

Address:

Malmöhusvägen 6, 211 18 Malmö

What people say

"A fascinating glimpse into Malmö's history." - Maria, Spain.

Lilla Torg

This charming square in the heart of Malmö's old town is lined with historic buildings, cafes, and restaurants. It's a popular spot for tourists and locals alike to relax, people-watch, and enjoy a meal or a drink.

Address:

Lilla torg, Malmö
